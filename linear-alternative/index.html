Where Linear Falls Short

What teams hit when they outgrow Linear

📅

No native timeline view

Linear has no built-in Gantt or timeline view. Roadmap planning requires workarounds or third-party tools, creating a disconnect between issues and long-term plans.

🔐

Basic role management

Linear offers Admin and Member roles — no project-level RBAC. When you need to give a contractor read-only access to one project, or restrict junior devs from certain boards, Linear can't do it.

🏢

Limited multi-team structure

Linear's Teams concept works well for 1–2 teams. Managing 5+ teams with different workflows, sprint cadences, and access levels becomes fragmented in Linear's workspace model.

💸

Per-member pricing

Linear charges per member at ~$8/month each. As you scale from 10 to 30 to 50 team members, costs grow linearly. Projiq's org-level pricing keeps costs predictable at any size.

🔧

Opinionated workflows

Linear's opinionated defaults are great at small scale but can become limiting. Custom issue statuses, complex workflow states, and org-specific processes are harder to configure.

📊

Limited reporting for leadership

Linear is optimized for individual engineers, not engineering managers or executives who need cross-team visibility, roadmap progress, and delivery tracking across multiple projects.

Projiq is the best Linear alternative for engineering teams that have outgrown Linear's limitations. Why do teams switch from Linear?
Teams most commonly outgrow Linear when they need: (1) timeline or Gantt views for roadmap planning; (2) granular RBAC for contractors, clients, or sub-teams with different access; (3) multi-team organization management at scale; (4) org-level pricing that doesn't grow per seat. Linear is excellent for small senior teams — Projiq is for when you scale.
Does Projiq have the same speed and real-time sync as Linear?
Yes. Projiq uses WebSocket-powered real-time sync — board updates push instantly to every team member, just like Linear's live sync. The interface is built to be fast and responsive for daily engineering use.
Is Projiq more expensive than Linear?
