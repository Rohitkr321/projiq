Why Teams Leave Jira

Common Jira frustrations

⚙️

Too complex to maintain

Jira requires a dedicated admin to configure workflows, permissions, and schemes. Most teams spend more time managing Jira than shipping product.

💸

Per-seat pricing at scale

Every new hire adds to the monthly bill. A 30-person team on Jira Standard costs $245/month — and that number grows with every engineer you hire.

🐌

Slow, dated interface

Jira's UI was designed for a different era. Simple tasks like moving an issue or editing a sprint require too many clicks and page loads.

🔧

Configuration before use

New Jira projects require workflows, issue types, screens, and schemes before any work can begin. Getting a team productive takes days, not minutes.

📊

Reporting that's hard to read

Built-in Jira reports are dense and hard to interpret without training. Most teams end up exporting data to spreadsheets just to get a clear picture.

🔒

Vendor lock-in risk

Deep Atlassian ecosystem dependency (Confluence, Bitbucket, Statuspage) makes switching painful. Teams stay on Jira out of inertia, not preference.

Jira alternative FAQ

What is the best Jira alternative for engineering teams?
Projiq is built specifically as a Jira alternative for engineering organizations of 5 to 100 people. Why do teams switch from Jira?
The three most common reasons teams leave Jira: (1) complexity — it requires a dedicated admin to maintain workflows and permissions; (2) cost — per-seat pricing grows with every hire; (3) UI friction — simple tasks require too many clicks and the interface feels dated compared to modern tools.
